Visualising location data obtained by using the Aware framework on an Android smartphone

Location visualization over a span of 2 weeks

The visualization above shows the location data collected by my Android device over a span of 2 weeks.


We observe the following

  • The location points all lie mainly in CMU, at home, or in the route between CMU and home
  • This data can be corroborated by my own experience during the hectic mid-semester week. I had expected the location points to lie close to each other, in and around CMU, which is observed from the visualization
  • The density of location points (that were collected with a fixed specified frequency) also points to another interesting inference. It tells us the places on the route from Walnut St. to CMU, places that had high traffic, and slower speeds as compared to places that had less traffic and therefore higher speed ot travel.
  • It can also be inferred that I take the same route on most days when travelling from and to CMU.

Vizualising the Altitude data over the days


From the above altitude visualization data, we can infer:

  • There is no data available over particular days. It might be a battery-optimization forcing the altitude sensor to switch off, or that the sensor data was not collected properly
  • The altitude data points are spread over a considerably large range, owing to the geographic nature of Pittsburgh
